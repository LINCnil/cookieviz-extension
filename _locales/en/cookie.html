A cookie is a small file stored by a server in the terminal (computer, telephone, etc.) of a user and associated with a web domain (i.e. in the majority of cases with all the pages of same website).

Cookies are read or deposited when using a terminal.



They make it possible to collect browsing data and identify a person when they consult a website, a mobile application, software, etc.

READING

Cookies can be placed in two different ways :

1. “First party” cookies (internal), read or write by the current website .

2. “Third parties” cookies, read or write other than the current website. Third party cookies are often used for advertising purposes.


USES

In all cases, cookies can be classified into two main categories: :

1. Cookies for the proper functioning of the service or the authentication of the user in order to remain connected. They are not subject to user consent

2. All other cookies, which make it possible to memorize data and create a detailed profile of the user, to track navigation, for example, thanks to the products viewed, the links clicked or the time spent on a page. These cookies are subject to the consent of the user who is free to accept them or not.

Whether it is a “First” cookie or a “Third” cookie, the purpose of the cookie is the only criterion for knowing whether consent is required.

INFORMATION

The consent to which certain cookies are subject requires informing the user about the use of cookies. The information is reflected by the presence of a cookie banner when opening a web page. The information must be of good quality and understandable by all for the consent to be considered valid.

COOKIE BANNER

The cookie banner could be structured like this:

Continue without accepting

Anything other than “accepting” should be considered a refusal.

In this area, must appear:

- Why cookies are stored (we are talking about purposes).

- Who store and reads cookies (we are talking about data controllers, partners or any other third party).

- How to control your data, in particular by being able to withdraw your consent at any time, in an accessible way.
